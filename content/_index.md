# The Project

## Description

This website is a personal project of mine that aims to make topics in
introductory math and physics courses less opaque.  For a while now, I
have been very frustrated with the general stigma that I feel both of
these subjects carry with them (e.g., "you have to be a genius to be
good at math/physics"), mostly because I just don't think that they're
true.

Let's take math as an example. Sure, math might not be for everyone---
it _can_ be a very challenging subject, and lots of people just don't
see a point to spending time making statements about abstract objects
they probably won't encounter in the "real world."  That's perfectly
fine, and I think that looking down at that kind of worldview (which
some mathematicians that I've met do) is pretty close-minded and
pretentious. I'll admit: I love smugly poking fun at Engineers as
much as any other prospective&trade; math/physics major, but I
certainly don't think that studying something like Engineering,
Classics, Medicine, Music, or Art is in some way "less" than pursuing
math.  My issue is that I feel most people don't even get a chance to
learn "what" math is
